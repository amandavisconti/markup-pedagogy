Other Encoding Notes

Mary vs. Percy! (recognizing hands) Percy's i's have dots that follow them, Mary's are right over the i Mary's final d's are looped Mary's r's are topped, Percy's r's are point or look like print r Mary writes very flat r's on top and loops her d's Percy's writing tends to be more slanted than Mary's General TEI Notes: We’re generally trying to encode things that aren’t presentational (so, not “there are quote marks here and there”, but “this whole block is a quotation” and someone will come along later and say “oh, quote blocks should be encased visually in quote marks”). “The encoding of documents for SGA privileges recording information about the physical document rather than about a ‘text’”: treat each page as a file existing alone in the universe, not as part of a greater novel (SGA schema). So, record physical elements (lines on page) and use milestone to show things like page and paragraph ends E.g. for paragraph ends, put just before the closing tag Don’t forget to always close your tags Cntrl + e surrounds the text you’ve highlighted with the beginning and ending tag of your choice If you find mistakes in the transcription, make a note in the XML doc. so Neil can know and verify it later. This information can be really valuable. Do double check to make sure they are mistakes.
